Even as Minnesota gradually reopens, daily life remains altered

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune June 22, 2020

After more than a month of shelter-in-place and two weeks gradual rollbacks of COVID-19 lockdowns, Minnesotans are socially distancing less and more engaged with summertime activities.

Yet with many summer events canceled for the year and several restrictions still in place, normalcy can still seem far off.

Data reveal some ways Minnesotans have started returning to normal routines.

Some things are normalizing

Minnesotans have been slacking off social distancing for a while, long before lockdowns started easing, so reclaiming normal traffic volume and daily movements didn't take long.

After weeks of creeping back toward normal levels, Minnesota's roadways are closer to normal daily activity levels.

On the whole, Minnesotans are back to typical isolation levels as well, according to Unacast data that measures social distancing. Minnesota's grade had been sliding gradually from a B+ when shelter-in-place started in March, reached a D- just before it ended, and now, like much of the United States, sits at an F.

Some things remain altered

Even though transit routes have started opening back up, some restrictings and service reductions remain across the Twin Cities metro area. That, combined with continued work-from-home arrangements, seem to still be depressing ridership on buses and light rail.

Bars and restaurants will still be missing crowds for a long while too, since even with the reopening of outdoor roof and patio dining, restrictions on capacity and required reservations seem have kept patrons eating at home.
